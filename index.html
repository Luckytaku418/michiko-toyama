01

A life across continents

1912
Osaka
Born in Osaka, Japan
Born in Osaka to Sutezo and Haru (Haruko) Toyama, her story begins in Japan, where music and curiosity about sound took root from the earliest years.
1930s
Paris
Studied under Nadia Boulanger — École Normale de Musique
Trained by the legendary pedagogue Nadia Boulanger, whose students included Copland, Bernstein, and Piazzolla. Michiko earned her place among the world's most rigorously trained composers.
École Normale de Musique, Paris
1937
Paris
Won the ISCM Prize — "The Voice of Yamato"
Her composition "The Voice of Yamato" won the International Society for Contemporary Music prize in Paris — making her the first Asian person and first woman ever to receive the honour. The work would not be heard in Japan for another 50 years.
Historic first · ISCM Paris 1937
1939
Paris
Baptised in Paris — then returned to Japan
Just before war broke out in Europe, Michiko was baptised in Paris — a profound act of faith at the edge of catastrophe. She then returned to Japan in 1939, leaving behind the world she had built over nearly a decade in France.
Paris, 1939
1940s
Japan
Married Kazuo — mother of Junko and Masashi
Back in Japan, she married Kazuo and built a family — their daughter Junko and son Masashi. The war took Kazuo from them. Left a widow with two young children, she faced grief and hardship with the same resolve that had carried her across continents.
Post-war
Paris
Returned to Paris — Milhaud & Messiaen, and Pierre Schaeffer
In a remarkable act of determination, she returned to Paris after the war to resume her studies at the Paris National Conservatory of Music under Darius Milhaud and Olivier Messiaen. It was there that she encountered Pierre Schaeffer — the father of musique concrète — who introduced her to the world of electronic music, setting the course for everything that followed.
Paris National Conservatoire · Pierre Schaeffer
1955
Tanglewood
Studied under Roger Sessions — Tanglewood Music Center
She continued her studies with the eminent American composer Roger Sessions at the Tanglewood Music Center in Massachusetts, further expanding her reach across continents and traditions.
Tanglewood Music Center, 1955
1956
New York
First woman invited to the Columbia-Princeton Electronic Music Center
In a field entirely dominated by men, Michiko was the first woman ever invited to work at the Columbia-Princeton Electronic Music Center in New York — one of the world's foremost laboratories for avant-garde and electronic composition.
Historic first · Columbia-Princeton EMC, 1956
Carnegie
Hall
Performed at Carnegie Hall
She took the podium at the world's most celebrated concert hall — a recognition of her singular gifts as a composer and conductor.
1960
Folkways
Album released — "Waka and Other Compositions" on Smithsonian Folkways
Her record Waka and Other Compositions: Contemporary Music of Japan (FW 8881) was released by Folkways Records — a landmark album of contemporary Japanese music that integrates electronic techniques and chamber orchestra with traditional poetry and themes.
Folkways Records FW 8881
1960s+
Japan
Returned to Japan — became an acoustic physicist
Back in Japan, she turned her trained musician's ear toward science. She analysed the acoustic properties of the Shakuhachi (traditional Japanese bamboo flute), publishing a series of scholarly papers across international conferences from 1971 through the 1980s.
Shakuhachi acoustics research
~1987
"The Voice of Yamato" performed in Japan — 50 years later
Half a century after her historic ISCM prize, her composition was finally heard in her homeland. A moment of profound, long-delayed homecoming.
50 years in the making
2006
Passed away, aged 94
A life of 94 years that spanned Japan, France, and America — concert halls, scientific laboratories, and every kind of barrier a woman of her time could face. She found a way through every one.

03

Acoustic research — the Shakuhachi papers

Over nearly two decades, she developed increasingly sophisticated acoustic models of the Shakuhachi — from spectral analysis of wet and dry bamboo tone, to ARMA synthesis models, to pole-zero modelling. A composer who became a scientist without ever ceasing to be a musician.

A life in photographs

"When you face a challenge, you find a way."

War & loss
WWII took her husband and upended her world. She rebuilt her life and career across two continents, alone, with two children.
Barriers of race & gender
As an Asian woman in 1930s–50s Europe and America, she walked through doors no one had opened before her — and left them open.
A second life in science
At an age when most rest on their achievements, she reinvented herself as a researcher — bringing a composer's ear to acoustic physics.
